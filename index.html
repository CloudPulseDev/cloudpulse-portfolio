Problem & Solution

Engineering teams are flying blind during outages

When a cloud incident fires, on-call engineers juggle multiple provider status pages, interpret vague alerts, and manually triage impact — all while the clock ticks and revenue bleeds.

Today

Teams monitor AWS, Azure, and OpenAI on separate status pages. Engineers spend 30–60 min on triage alone before any fix begins. Alert fatigue and inconsistent escalations are routine.

With CloudPulse

One unified feed. AI classifies severity, maps affected services, and outputs copy-paste CLI commands for immediate action. Weekly reports surface patterns before they escalate.

Product

One intelligent dashboard for all your cloud incidents

Aggregates real-time incident data across the three most critical cloud providers and applies AI to turn raw alerts into actionable intelligence.

Amazon Web Services

OpenAI
01 — Monitoring
Unified Incident Feed

All provider incidents in a single filterable dashboard. No tab-switching. AWS, Azure, and OpenAI — live, in one place.

02 — AI Analysis
Root Cause & Severity Triage

Plain-language summaries explaining what's broken and why. Automatic P1–P4 classification so teams prioritize from second one.

03 — Remediation
Copy-Paste CLI Commands

Context-aware, ready-to-run CLI commands delivered with every incident. Engineers act in seconds, not minutes.

04 — Intelligence
Weekly Trend Reports

Trending failure patterns, worst-performing providers, and service hotspots — surfaced automatically each week.

05 — Optional Add-on
Infrastructure Mapping & Revenue Impact

Connect your own infrastructure to map incidents directly to your services. Estimates revenue impact, surfaces dependency chains, and generates context-aware recovery plans tailored to your specific stack — turning generic incident data into business-critical intelligence.
